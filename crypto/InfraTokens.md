## TL;DR

- **Price everything in BTC, not USD.** BTC is the hurdle rate (Lyn Alden). An alt only "wins" if it
  beats holding BTC.
- **The base rate is brutal:** 2013→2017 only **2 of 20** top alts made a new BTC-denominated high;
  2017→2021 **0 of 20**. Median top-300 alt falls **−90% vs BTC within 10–20 months.** Only ~2–3 alts
  in history (ETH, BNB, maybe SOL) durably beat BTC.
- **Of your four, only HYPE is actually beating BTC** (+~2.9× vs BTC in 12mo). JUP −41%, PUMP −49%,
  ASTER mixed/unverifiable — all losing to BTC.
- **The filter that works:** a token needs a *real, organic, enforced* fee→holder value-accrual
  mechanism. HYPE has it (~97% of fees buy back HYPE). ASTER/PUMP don't (reflexive + unverifiable).
- **Verdict:** BTC = core. HYPE = the one defensible infra hold (already +62%, don't add at ~20×).
  AAVE = small clean-cashflow value bet. Skip/avoid ASTER, PUMP, JUP, TRUMP.

## The Lyn Alden lens (validated)

From lynalden.com: *"Utility protocols face heavy competition, and thus their value trends toward
their marginal cost of operation… it will be the **users** that primarily benefit rather than the
long-term coinholders."* Most tokens = "speculation at best." She does **not** grant cashflow tokens a
DCF lens. **Honest counter:** when fees are *programmatically* routed to the token (HYPE's enforced
buyback), it behaves like an equity claim on an exchange — and exchange earnings (CME, Coinbase) are
cyclical too without being "fake." But even the bull concedes the *median* alt loses ~99% vs BTC.

## The HYPE bear case (so the one exception stays honest)
1. Priced for permanence — FDV ~$35–57B already ≈ the bull-case target.
2. Buyback is a **reflexive cushion, not a floor** — volume (and the bid) shrinks exactly when unlocks hit.
3. Assistance Fund is **governance-controlled, not immutable** (validators already voted on changing it).
4. Centralization — small validator set, has manually delisted/overridden prices.
5. Competition (Aster/CZ-backed, dYdX, Drift) + perp-DEX regulatory target.
6. Revenue cyclical, **untested through a deep bear at scale.**

## The reusable filter (= the "is it a real infra token?" test)
1. **Organic** fee revenue (not incentivized/wash) that is **growing**.
2. **Enforced** fee→token accrual (automatic buyback/burn), not discretionary.
3. **Category leadership** / durable moat.
4. **Beats BTC** (BTC-denominated uptrend).
5. **Clean-ish FDV / low forward dilution.**
6. **Durable through a bear** (revenue not purely reflexive).

HYPE is the only one of the set that clears all six. **Next goal:** use this exact filter to hunt
early projects that could become the *next* HYPE — see `crypto/next-hype-candidates.md`.
