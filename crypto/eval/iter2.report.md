- COIN at ~2-3x beta means a −50% BTC move plausibly takes COIN down **−60% to −80%**. On a 30% book weight, that alone is a **−18% to −24% hit to the entire book** *before any added BTC.*
- The discipline: crypto risk is drawn from the ~$0.5M risk-capital bucket only, and the **$1.5M house reserve is untouchable** (your standing rule). Any BTC tranche must be sized so that COIN + BTC together, stressed at −50%, stays *inside* the $0.5M sleeve and never reaches into the reserve. From the numbers in the brief, the COIN position is already consuming much of that sleeve's stress budget. **The binding constraint is your existing concentration, not the BTC entry price.**

### 4. Is a tranche scale-in sound?
Behaviorally, yes — *if* the prior three points hold. Tranching is the procedural form of "room for error": it removes the single-day timing bet (the exact trap in this question), it survives being wrong about the bottom, and it keeps you in the game so time can compound. It directly defuses the FOMC anchoring trap — you don't need to be right about June 17 if you're only deploying a fraction. The caveat Housel insists on: **tranching a position that is already oversized is still oversizing.** A scale-in is a discipline for *entering*, not a license to *add to* an already-extended factor bet. The sound version is small tranches against a pre-defined total BTC target that — combined with COIN — keeps the whole crypto sleeve survivable at −50%.

### 5. One guardrail rule
**Set the total crypto-sleeve size FIRST (COIN + BTC together, stress-tested at −50% to stay inside the $0.5M risk bucket and never touch the $1.5M reserve); only then decide if there's room for a BTC tranche — and if there is, fund it by trimming COIN, not by adding factor.** Survival is the whole game: don't risk what you have and need (the reserve, the un-levered book) for what you don't have and don't need (more of an exposure you already carry at 2-3x).
