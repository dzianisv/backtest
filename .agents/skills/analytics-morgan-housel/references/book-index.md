# Book Index & Provenance — *The Psychology of Money*
> Source: Morgan Housel, *The Psychology of Money: Timeless Lessons on Wealth, Greed, and Happiness* (2020, Harriman House). Publisher page: https://harriman-house.com/thepsychologyofmoney. Distilled 2026-06.

This skill is a **distillation of principles from the book** — Housel's behavioral-finance / investor-psychology framework — *not* a substitute for reading it and *not* investment advice. The book is 20 short, mostly self-contained chapters plus an introduction and a postscript on U.S. consumer history. Each thematic reference file in this skill maps a cluster of those chapters; load the relevant one before any load-bearing claim, and cite the chapter.

## Honesty / scope notes
- **Principles, not prescriptions.** Housel explicitly declines to tell readers *what* to buy or do ("I'm not going to tell you what to do with your money"); the book is about behavior and process. Do not derive specific trades, tickers, or allocations from this skill.
- **Some figures are as-of-2020** (e.g., Buffett's $84.5bn net worth, oil-production numbers, the "85% of large-cap active managers lagged the S&P over the decade ending 2019" stat). Treat them as illustrative of the *principle*, not current data.
- Quotes and examples in the reference files are drawn from the book's own text and the sources Housel cites within it.
