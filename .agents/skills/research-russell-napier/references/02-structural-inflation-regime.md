# Structural Inflation Regime — Russell Napier's Framework
> Sources: The Market/NZZ financial-repression interview (2020-07-14), capex-boom interview (2022-10-14), national-capitalism interview (2024-12); ByteTree webinar note (bytetree.medium.com); MacroVoices "Knock-on Effects of Secular Inflation" (macrovoices.com). Distilled 2026-06-07.

## Core thesis
The 40-year disinflation (1980-2020) is over; we have entered a structural, multi-year regime of elevated inflation that Napier pegs at roughly 4-6% in the developed world, sustained not by central-bank error but by deliberate government policy. Inflation is the chosen tool to deleverage record debt. It is reinforced by real-economy forces — deglobalization/reshoring, defense and energy capex, decarbonization, demographics — that all raise the cost structure and demand for capital. Crucially, financial repression keeps nominal rates BELOW inflation, so real rates stay negative for years; this is a feature, not a policy failure. He expects this to run ~15-20 years, the time needed to bring debt/GDP back under control.

## Key frameworks / mental models
- **End of the 40-yr disinflation** — the China/globalization disinflationary tailwind reverses into a structural headwind.
- **Inflation as deleveraging tool** — sustained 4-6% inflation + capped rates = quiet debt destruction.
- **Negative real rates by design** — the gap between inflation and policy rate is the repression "tax."
- **Broad-money master gauge** — >10% broad-money growth underwrites >4% inflation.
- **Capex-boom cost-push** — reshoring, defense, energy/grid, friendshoring drive a sustained investment cycle that is inflationary (largest since 1994).
- **Technology doesn't defeat it** — productivity/AI cannot offset government-driven money growth and structural capex (his 2026 framing).

## Specific claims, mechanisms & data
- Inflation forecast: "consumer price inflation settling into a range between 4 and 6%" (2022-10); earlier "between 4 and 5.5%" over the next decade (2020-07).
- Broad money: "settle at around 10%" growth; rule of thumb — broad money >10% => inflation stabilizes >4%.
- Nominal GDP engineered to ~6-8% to outgrow debt; rates held below that.
- Drivers (his four-to-five): "energy, climate change, defence, inequality, our dependence on production from China — will all be solved by massive investment."
- Duration: "at least a decade and a half" to get debt under control; bond yields "will not be a free-market price anymore for at least 15 years."
- A 4% sustained inflation regime implies, per a ByteTree reading of his work, very high gold (cited ~$7,000) once the market reprices real rates.
- Near-term caveat he keeps flagging: a collapse in broad-money growth "in a magnitude not seen since the 1930s" — a deflationary air-pocket before the inflation regime dominates.

## How to APPLY (decision rules for an agent using this lens)
1. Anchor base-case inflation at 4-6%, not 2%; treat target-level inflation as the surprise, not the norm.
2. Set expected real policy rates negative; do NOT assume rates rise one-for-one with inflation — repression caps them.
3. Use broad-money growth as the leading inflation indicator; cross-check against bank-lending and guarantee programs.
4. Treat reshoring/defense/energy capex as a persistent cost-push floor under inflation — fade "inflation is transitory / tech will fix it" narratives.
5. Distinguish a cyclical deflation scare (buy real assets into it) from the end of the regime (would require restored CB control of money — not his base case).

## Caveats / where he hedges
- He was wrong/early for years: famously deflationist ~1995-2019, so a structural-inflation call from him should be weighted for his bullish-on-inflation BIAS post-2020.
- He himself concedes serious near-term DEFLATION risk from collapsing broad money — the regime is a multi-year average, not a smooth path; CPI can undershoot 4-6% for stretches.
- Mainstream view: 2022-24 disinflation back toward 2-3% (as CB hikes bit and energy normalized) is direct counter-evidence; many economists see 2-3% as the new normal, not 4-6%.
- He concedes timing is unknowable — "I don't know the timing" — and that the regime depends on continued political will to repress.

- "If broad money growth stabilises above 10% per annum, then I don't have a problem saying inflation stabilises above 4%."

- "Energy, climate change, defence, inequality, our dependence on production from China — will all be solved by massive investment."
