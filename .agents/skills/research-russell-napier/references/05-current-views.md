# Current Views (2023-2026) — Russell Napier's Framework
> Sources: Meb Faber Show #615 (themebfabershow.com, 2026-01-30); The Market/NZZ national-capitalism interview (themarket.ch, 2024-12); Hidden Forces "National Capitalism & Death of the International Monetary System" (hiddenforces.io, recorded 2024-11-27); MacroVoices #1015 (macrovoices.com, 2021-10-07); ByteTree webinar note; Behind the Balance Sheet substack. Distilled 2026-06-07. Every claim dated. The Market may be metered/paywalled.

## Core thesis
As of 2026 Napier holds that the financial-repression / structural-inflation regime is now the operative reality, NOT a forecast: power over money creation has moved from central banks to governments, the post-1994 international monetary system is "collapsing in front of our eyes," and the dominant macro story is a transition to "national capitalism" in which states direct national savings to national ends and erode debt via inflation > rates over ~15-20 years. He frames investors as "asking the wrong questions at a critical turning point" and reads gold's strength as the market confirming the regime. He continues to flag a NEAR-TERM deflation/recession risk (collapsing broad money) sitting inside the longer inflation regime.

## Key frameworks / mental models
- **Regime, not cycle** — treat repression + 4-6% inflation as a structural 15-20 yr regime; stop extrapolating the 1980-2020 disinflation.
- **National capitalism** — governments direct/coerce private savings into defense, energy, reshoring; capital controls follow.
- **Monetary-system collapse on a ~30-40 yr clock** — the system since 1994 is ending now; gold is the beneficiary.
- **Gold as monetary signal** — its move is information about the regime, not just a hedge.
- **"Technology doesn't defeat inflation"** — fade the AI/productivity-will-fix-inflation thesis (2026 framing).

## How to APPLY (decision rules for an agent using this lens)
1. Default to the regime view: assume governments (fiscal/credit guarantees) set the monetary impulse, gold is structurally bid, and long bonds are confiscation risk.
2. Watch for confirming policy events: pension/insurer mandates to hold govt debt, new/extended loan-guarantee schemes, capital controls, defense/energy directed-credit — each ups conviction.
3. Treat gold strength as thesis confirmation; treat a sharp broad-money contraction as a deflation-scare warning (tactical caution, not regime reversal).
4. On the USD, hold his view as time-varying: strong-dollar-first (2021) has been superseded by gold-first as repression spreads — re-derive from current rate-differential and repression sequencing, don't hard-code.
5. Prefer non-US, value, small/mid, and capex-boom equities; minimize long-duration sovereign exposure; size gold as a core holding.

## Caveats / where he hedges
- He has been EARLY for years (deflationist to ~2019; structural-inflation call from 2020-21) — current-view timing should be treated as a multi-year regime, not a this-year trade.
- His own near-term deflation warnings can contradict the headline inflation regime; he hedges that the path is bumpy and timing "unknowable."
- His USD call has shifted (strong-dollar 2021 → gold-first 2024-26); cross-check the live data rather than quoting an old directional view.
- Mainstream disagreement: 2023-24 disinflation toward 2-3%, resilient US equities/USD, and recovered 60/40 are live counter-evidence; many see CB hikes as still binding inflation.
