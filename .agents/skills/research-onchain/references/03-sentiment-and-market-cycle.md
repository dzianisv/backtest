# Sentiment & Market Cycle — The Contrarian Modulator (the mood)
> Source: Alternative.me Crypto Fear & Greed Index; market-cycle / rainbow-chart heuristics (popular crypto-content sites); mapped to Howell's four-phase liquidity cycle (`01`). Distilled 2026-06. Sentiment data is live — re-pull.

## Core thesis
Crowd emotion is **mean-reverting at extremes**, so sentiment is read **contrarily**: peak fear clusters near bottoms, peak greed near tops. It is the **mood** layer — a *modulator* that widens or narrows the valuation tilt, never a standalone trigger. Its job is to tell you when the crowd has over-shot the on-chain *level* in either direction, and it maps cleanly onto Howell's four-phase liquidity cycle so the three pillars rhyme.

## Key frameworks / mental models
- **Crypto Fear & Greed Index (0–100).** Composite of volatility, momentum, volume, social media, dominance, trends. **Extreme fear (≈0–25) → accumulate; extreme greed (≈75–100) → trim the tilt / raise caution.** Buffett's "fearful/greedy" applied to crypto.
- **Market-cycle / rainbow charts.** Color-banded log-regression overlays (e.g. "fire sale" → "maximum bubble") — *heuristic visual context only*, not a model (they share S2F's regression-fitting weakness; treat as mood texture, not signal).
- **Four-phase cycle, mapped to Howell.** Accumulation ↔ **Calm** (liquidity high/rising, sentiment numb — bottoms form); Markup ↔ **Speculation** (liquidity high/falling, greed building); Distribution/Euphoria ↔ **Turbulence** (liquidity low/falling, extreme greed — tops form); Capitulation/Despair ↔ **Rebound** (liquidity low/rising, extreme fear — best accumulation). The mood confirms where the tide and the level say you are.

## Specific claims, mechanisms & data
- **Sentiment-tilted DCA** (buy more when F&G is in extreme fear) has, in published crypto backtests, beaten plain buy-and-hold on a **risk-adjusted** basis — reported **Sharpe ~1.38 vs ~0.88.**
- **HONESTY FLAG:** those Sharpe numbers are **self-reported by crypto-content sites**, not peer-reviewed, and are vulnerable to backtest overfitting and period-selection. Treat the *direction* (fear-tilt helps risk-adjusted) as plausible; treat the *magnitude* as unverified.

## How to APPLY (decision rules for the modulator)
1. **Overlay F&G on the on-chain zone (`02`).** Extreme fear *at* a cheap on-chain zone → **widen** the buy tilt (conviction the crowd over-shot down). Extreme greed *at* a rich zone → **shrink** the tilt / consider trimming.
2. **Resolve disagreement toward the slower signal.** If sentiment and on-chain disagree (e.g. greed but cheap on-chain), defer to on-chain + liquidity; sentiment alone is the weakest pillar.
3. **Cross-check the phase.** Confirm the F&G read against the Howell phase (`01`) and the cycle map above — coherent across pillars = higher-conviction tilt.
4. **Modulate, don't trigger.** Sentiment changes the *size* of a DCA tranche, never whether to abandon the schedule.

## Caveats / where it hedges
- **Sentiment can stay extreme** far longer than expected (greed persisted through entire bull legs) — contrarian ≠ instant reversal.
- **Rainbow/cycle charts are fitted overlays**, not predictive models — context, not signal.
- **The headline Sharpe figures are unverified marketing numbers** — don't quote them as established fact.
- **Weakest of the four pillars** — use as a modulator on top of liquidity + on-chain, never alone.
