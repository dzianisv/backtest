# Analytics: The Ray Dalio Lens

Apply Ray Dalio's framework to a question. This skill is the **synthesis + router**; the detailed
reasoning lives in `references/` (one file per theme). Load the relevant reference before making a
load-bearing claim — don't reason from this summary alone.

## The unifying worldview (everything connects to this)

Dalio reasons **mechanistically and historically**: the economy is a machine made of transactions
(spending = money + credit), and the same cause→effect patterns repeat across cycles and across
empires. Three forces stack: a rising **productivity** trendline, a **short-term debt cycle** (~5-8yr,
the business cycle the Fed manages), and a **long-term/big debt cycle** (~75yr ±) that ends when debt
claims grow far larger than the real output to service them — at which point the central bank always
**prints and devalues**. Zoom out further and there is a ~250yr **Big Cycle** of empires: a nation
rises, wins reserve-currency status, over-borrows in that currency, and declines as a rival rises.
Position implication: study where you are in *all* the cycles, **balance risk not dollars** across
environments, and hold debasement insurance (gold/real assets) because the endgame is devaluation.

## Core mental models (the load-bearing ones)

1. **The economy = sum of transactions; credit is the swing factor.** Reason up from spending (money
   + credit), not from aggregates. Credit creates debt instantly and drives the short run; productivity
   drives the long run. → `references/01-economic-machine.md`
2. **Three forces:** productivity trend + short-term debt cycle + long-term debt cycle. Locate the
   question in all three. → `references/01-economic-machine.md`
3. **Big debt cycle endgame = print + devalue.** When claims exceed serviceable output, the central
   bank monetizes; the risk to Treasuries is "default or devalue — more likely devalue."
   → `references/02-big-debt-cycles.md`
4. **Four deleveraging levers; beautiful vs ugly.** Austerity + default are deflationary; printing +
   redistribution are inflationary. A "beautiful deleveraging" balances them so nominal growth > rates
   with contained inflation. → `references/02-big-debt-cycles.md`
5. **The Big Cycle of empires (~250yr), scored by the 8 types of power.** Reserve-currency status is
   the prize that seeds the decline. → `references/03-changing-world-order.md`
6. **Three disorder forces:** the debt/money cycle + the internal wealth/values gap + external
   great-power conflict. Dalio places the world in "Stage 5." → `references/03-changing-world-order.md`
7. **The Holy Grail of diversification:** ~15 *uncorrelated* return streams cut risk ~80% at the same
   return. Correlation matters more than count. → `references/04-all-weather-risk-parity.md`
8. **All-Weather / the four economic boxes:** balance **risk, not dollars**, across growth↑↓ ×
   inflation↑↓; never leave a box empty. → `references/04-all-weather-risk-parity.md`
9. **Gold/real assets as debasement insurance** (~10-15%); reduce single-currency overexposure; "cash
   is trash" over the long run (but not always short-run). → `references/04-all-weather-risk-parity.md`
10. **Pain + Reflection = Progress; believability-weighting; expected-value thinking.** Weight views by
    track record × logic; make the worst case survivable. → `references/05-principles-and-current.md`

## How to apply the lens (decision procedure)

1. **Locate the question in the cycles first.** Short-term debt cycle (where in the business cycle)?
   Big debt cycle (early/mid/late, pre- or post-monetization)? Big Cycle (which stage of empire/reserve
   decline)? State the cycle read.
2. **Load the matching reference(s)** (routing table) and ground claims in his mechanics + historical
   analogues (1930s vs 1940s, pre-1945, etc.).
3. **Frame it as a machine:** who is spending, on credit or money, and what does that do to growth,
   inflation, and the debt burden next?
4. **Give the balanced-portfolio implication, not a single bet.** Dalio's answer is almost always
   "balance the exposures across environments + hold debasement insurance," not "buy X."
5. **Separate durable framework from dated tactical view.** His evergreen mechanics are the framework;
   his "current positioning" decays — for "what does he think now", read `05-principles-and-current.md`
   and re-check his latest LinkedIn/X essay (don't quote a 2024 stance as today's).

## Example

<example>
User: "The US deficit is ~6-7% of GDP and debt keeps climbing — how does this end?"

Lens response: "Through Dalio's big-debt-cycle lens (load `02-big-debt-cycles.md` + `03-changing-world-order.md`):
this is late-cycle. When debt claims outgrow serviceable output, the resolution is never clean default —
the central bank prints and the currency devalues; the risk to long Treasuries is devaluation, not
non-payment. His base case is a managed 'beautiful deleveraging' IF policymakers balance austerity/
default (deflationary) against printing/redistribution (inflationary) — and he's loudly skeptical they
will, hence the '3% solution' deficit warning and his 'Stage 5' read. Portfolio implication: don't be
overexposed to the debtor's currency or its long bonds; balance risk across the four boxes and hold
~10-15% gold as debasement insurance. Hedge: his debt-crisis timing has repeatedly been early — this is
a regime map, not a near-term trade trigger."
</example>

## Honesty rules (non-negotiable)

- **It's a lens, not gospel.** Present it as "Dalio's framework says…". Note where others disagree.
- **He has been wrong / early.** "Cash is trash" (2019) preceded a cash-spike in the 2020 crash; his
  debt-crisis warnings have run years early; perma-themes can underperform for a long time. Carry the
  caveats from each reference's Caveats section.
- **Current positioning decays.** Date every "current view" claim and re-check his latest essay.
- **Ground load-bearing claims** in a specific reference/source (via `references/article-index.md`).
- This skill **complements** `investor-lyn-alden` (shares the debasement endgame) and the rest of the
  macro panel; it feeds `portfolio-construction` / `regime-detection`. It does not replace the
  `fundamental-analysis` valuation gate or your own analysis.

## Done when

The analysis (1) locates the question in the relevant cycle(s), (2) is grounded in the right reference
with a primary-source pointer for load-bearing claims, (3) reasons through the machine (money/credit →
growth/inflation/debt), (4) gives a balanced-portfolio implication plus debasement insurance, and
(5) flags any current/tactical claim as time-stamped.
