# Global Liquidity & Bitcoin — Howell's Framework (the tide)
> Source: Michael J. Howell, *Capital Wars: The Rise of Global Liquidity* (Palgrave Macmillan, 2020) — https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-3-030-39288-8 . Distilled 2026-06. Crypto-application layer added by this repo (flagged below).

## Core thesis
Asset prices are driven first by **the quantity and flow of money and credit** — *Global Liquidity* — not by interest rates or CPI. Liquidity is the gross flow of credit + savings + cross-border capital through banking systems and collateral-based wholesale money markets: a **source-of-funds** concept, not textbook money supply. In a world where debt is *refinanced* (rolled over) far more than newly issued, what matters is **balance-sheet capacity**, not the price of money. The Global Liquidity pool is enormous (~US$130tn, ~2/3 larger than World GDP) and **footloose** — it rotates between safe assets (cash, govt bonds) and risk assets (equities, EM, real estate, and — as a later, qualitative note — crypto). Because liquidity *leads* the risk cycle by months, it is the single most important governor of when to deploy. For crypto specifically: BTC behaves as the highest-beta sponge for this tide, so the liquidity read sits **above** every valuation/sentiment read. **(Scope flag: the high-beta-BTC framing and any "5–6 year cycle" are LATER Howell / CrossBorder Capital, not this 2020 book — see the Later Howell section below.)**

## Key frameworks / mental models
- **Capacity of capital > cost of capital.** Balance-sheet size, not interest rates, sets liquidity. "Liquidity cannot be properly measured by the level of interest rates." Funding liquidity (capacity) vs market liquidity (trade in size).
- **Markets are refinancing mechanisms.** Debt rollover dominates new issuance; repo (not deposits) is the marginal funding instrument; gross funding dwarfs net new credit.
- **The Global Liquidity Cycle leads the business/risk cycle.** Four phases by level/direction: **Rebound** (low/rising) → **Calm** (high/rising) → **Speculation** (high/falling) → **Turbulence** (low/falling). Risk assets **peak in Turbulence, bottom in Calm.**
- **Lead-lag (in MONTHS).** GLI leads bond/FX markets ~3–6 months, equity markets ~6–12 months, the real economy ~15–20 months; the yield curve flattens/steepens ~6–9 months after liquidity peaks/troughs.
- **Three liquidity rules.** "Don't Fight the Fed, Don't Upset the ECB, Don't Mess with the PBoC." USD is the world's funding currency via the offshore Eurodollar/repo system: **stronger USD = tightening, weaker USD = easing.**
- **The GLI composite.** Diffusion index of z-scores, 0–100 (mean 50; +1sd=60, +2sd=80, −1sd=40, −2sd=20) vs a **41-month trend**; >50 above-trend, <50 below-trend. Avg weights: **32% central-bank, 32% private, 20% cross-border, 16% short-term spreads.** ~30 series/economy, ~80 economies, monthly.

## Specific claims, mechanisms & data
- **Size.** Global Liquidity ≈ US$130tn (end-Jul-2019 ~$128.2tn), ~2/3 bigger than World GDP; total financial markets ~US$250tn ≈ 3.5× World GDP. Shadow banks ~2/3 of gross funding but <15% of new credit.
- **Plumbing.** Collateral multiplier fell from ~3.0× (2007) to ~1.8–2.0× (2016–17); GFC haircuts on ABS went ~4%→~60%; corporate/institutional cash pools >US$30tn; non-US-dollar corporate debt ~US$17tn (2/3 USD-denominated → rollover/maturity-wall risk); Sept-2019 repo spiked to 10%.
- **Central banks.** "Power, but not always control." QE empirically **raised** term premia (avg +134bp through past US QE phases — contrarian to consensus). Term premia drive ~77% of 10yr yield variation.
- **Crisis rhythm.** "Financial crises seem to strike with an apparent regularity every eight to ten years"; >60 countries had boom-bust since 1980.
- **Crypto (qualitative only in this book).** Howell notes a "shift towards alternative monies, such as Bitcoin, XRP, Ethereum" as a **signal of growing distrust** in banks/central banks — he does **not** model BTC as a high-beta liquidity asset or give a crypto-liquidity beta here.
- **Gold.** A safe asset but "not sufficiently elastic" for refinancing.

## How to APPLY (decision rules for the liquidity governor)
1. **Read the tide first.** Estimate GLI direction & level from its inputs/proxies: central-bank balance-sheet growth, broad credit/financial-savings growth, net cross-border flows, private leverage/collateral multipliers, short-term spreads (LIBOR-OIS/TED), and the USD (stronger = tightening).
2. **Map to a phase.** Rebound/Calm (rising) → **green-light, deploy faster**. Speculation (high/falling) → late-cycle, tilt cyclical but watch the rollover. Turbulence (low/falling) → **throttle to defensive tranches, raise cash.**
3. **Act ahead of price.** Liquidity leads equities/BTC ~6–12 months — deploy/withdraw *before* the move is obvious, not after.
4. **Use it as a governor, not a trigger.** It caps or releases the *pace* of DCA; it does not by itself say "buy today." Pair with on-chain (the level) and sentiment (the mood).
5. **Watch the USD and the big-three CBs** as the fastest-moving liquidity tells.

## Caveats / where it hedges
- **Leads are noisy and in months, not days** — this is a regime governor, not a timing oscillator.
- **Measuring GLI is hard** (the index is CrossBorder Capital's proprietary composite; public proxies are imperfect).
- **HONESTY FLAG — later-Howell vs this book.** *Capital Wars* (2020) does **NOT** state a clean "5–6 year liquidity cycle" — it frames cyclicality via crises "every 8–10 years," a 41-month trend baseline, and leads measured in months. It also does **NOT** quantify a Bitcoin liquidity-beta; crypto appears only qualitatively as a "distrust" signal. The popular **high-beta-BTC framing and the 5–6yr-cycle** are from **later Howell (CrossBorder Capital)** — cite them as such, not as claims of this 2020 book. **See the "Later Howell (post-2020)" section below** for the distilled post-book material.

## Later Howell (post-2020): the 65-month cycle & Bitcoin as liquidity proxy
> Source layer: Michael Howell / **CrossBorder Capital**, post-*Capital Wars* research — the **Capital Wars Substack** (capitalwars.substack.com) plus 2024–26 interviews (MacroVoices, Bankless, *Forward Guidance*, *Real Vision*). This is the material the 2020 book did **not** contain; it is distilled here from web sources, attributed inline, and kept separate from the book content above on purpose.

### The 65-month (≈5.4yr, "5–6 year") Global Liquidity Cycle
Howell's headline post-book refinement: the Global Liquidity Cycle runs **~65 months (≈5.4 years, the "5–6 year" cycle)**, and its length is **not arbitrary** — it equals the **average maturity of world debt**, so the liquidity cycle is really a **debt-refinancing cycle**. As debt comes due it must be rolled over; the wave of refinancing dictates the rhythm of liquidity expansion and contraction.
- Quote: *"The average maturity of debt in the world economy is almost exactly 65 months… what you're looking at is a debt refinancing cycle."*
- **Phases:** trough → rebound → calm → speculation → turbulence → peak → downswing. (A richer, named version of the 2020 book's four-phase Rebound/Calm/Speculation/Turbulence scheme, extended around the peak/downswing.)
- **Current placement (his 2024–26 calls):** cycle **peaked ~Q3 2025** (some appearances place the peak into **early 2026**); **early downswing through 2026**; **trough expected ~2027**; next **upswing 2028+**.
- (Source: *The 5-6 Year Re-fi Cycle*, Capital Wars Substack, 24 Jan 2025; reiterated in 2025–26 MacroVoices / Forward Guidance interviews.)

### Bitcoin as the highest-beta liquidity proxy (almost entirely post-book)
The 2020 book treats crypto only qualitatively (a "distrust" signal). The **quantified BTC-as-liquidity-proxy** work is post-book CrossBorder research:
- Global Liquidity is **"strongly Granger causal"** for Bitcoin — liquidity **leads** BTC, not the reverse.
- **Lead timing:** impact concentrated at **11–13 weeks** (earliest significant response ~**5 weeks**, latest ~**16 weeks**).
- **Driver share:** Global Liquidity ≈ **40%** of BTC's systematic drivers; **risk-appetite ≈ another ~22%**.
- **Functional form:** a **log-log power-law** relationship — diminishing marginal response as liquidity rises (each extra dollar of liquidity moves BTC less, in log terms).
- Signature quote: *"Bitcoin is the most liquidity-sensitive asset on the planet… a canary in the coal mine for tightening liquidity conditions. If liquidity goes down, you'll see it first in Bitcoin."*
- (Source: *What Drives Bitcoin?*, Capital Wars Substack, 18 Dec 2024; echoed in Bankless / Real Vision 2024–25 interviews.)
- **HONESTY FLAG — what is Howell vs. secondary framing.** The widely-circulated **"~0.9 correlation"** / **"~90% of Bitcoin's move is liquidity"** figure is **Raoul Pal / secondary commentary framing, NOT a precise Howell statistic.** Attribute to **Howell**: the **11–13-week lead** and the **~40%-of-variance** driver share. Attribute the **0.9 / 90%** number to **secondary commentary** — do not put it in Howell's mouth.

### The 2026 "tide going out" read & the debt wall
- **Liquidity inflecting lower** from its peak — the tide is going out near-term.
- **The debt wall:** total global debt **~$350tn** at a **~5-year average maturity** ⇒ the world must **refinance ~$70tn/year**. Howell notes **70–80% of financial-market transactions are now refinancing**, not new investment; the **2026–27 maturity wall steepens**.
- **Near-term BEARISH** for BTC/risk as liquidity drains — he has floated **BTC downside toward ~$30k in 2026**.
- **Structurally BULLISH into the ~2027 trough:** the refi wall **forces** eventual monetization/QE (the debt cannot be allowed to fail to roll), so liquidity must turn back up. Tactic he repeats: **"buy on weakness," ~20–25% below trend.**
- **Size update (post-book):** Global Liquidity **~$175tn (Sep 2024)** → **peak ~$188.8tn (early 2026)**, vs the **~$130tn** of the 2019 book era.
- (Sources: Capital Wars Substack 2025–26 posts; MacroVoices, *Forward Guidance*, and Bankless interviews, 2024–26.)
