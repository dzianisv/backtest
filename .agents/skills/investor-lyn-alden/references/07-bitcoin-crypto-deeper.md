## Core thesis
Bitcoin is the only genuinely decentralized monetary asset in the digital-asset space; almost everything else ("digital alchemy") trades decentralization for features and markets the trade-off as innovation. Two durable, separable claims run through the corpus: (1) **Bitcoin is a monetary asset (gold-like); smart-contract platform tokens are equity securities (oil-like)** — they should be valued and regulated differently, and ecosystem growth does NOT automatically capture token value. (2) **Bitcoin is the purest macro barometer of global liquidity** — among all major assets it tracks global M2 most faithfully because it has no earnings, no safe-haven bid, and no country risk to contaminate the signal. Around these she analyzes why most of crypto blew up in 2022 (leverage + custodial counterparty risk, not "DeFi vs CeFi"), why PoS and stablecoins re-introduce centralization, why corporate BTC treasuries (MSTR-style) are a rational and inevitable structural development, and whether Bitcoin's fee-based security budget can sustain itself post-subsidy.

## Key frameworks / mental models
- **Monetary asset vs. equity security.** Conceptualize BTC as money (gold-like, store of value), and ETH/SOL/etc. tokens as equity in a platform (oil-like utility). Most altcoins "pass the Howey test." This reframes "BTC vs alts" as a false dichotomy: the real comparison is decentralized money vs. centralized equity platforms.
- **Decentralization trade-off.** Bitcoin deliberately "sacrificed most metrics" (speed, features, throughput) to get automated, auditable, trustless consensus. Every feature an altcoin adds claws back some decentralization/security/auditability. Ask of any chain: *what is the fundamental compromise?*
- **Liquidity barometer.** Post-2008, "liquidity moves markets" (Druckenmiller). BTC = purest liquidity gauge because nothing confounds it (no earnings/401k flows like stocks, no safe-haven bid like gold/bonds, no country risk like EM).
- **MVRV Z-Score overlay.** On-chain valuation gauge (market value vs. realized cost basis). High = long-term holders sitting on profit, likely to sell; low = accumulation zone. Use to know WHEN the liquidity correlation will break (profit-taking overrides macro at bubble peaks).
- **The dilution problem (treasury).** Any savings asset must outpace money-supply growth (~7%/yr developed) or it dilutes. Cash/T-bills lose; gold dilutes slowly (~1-2%/yr new supply); BTC has zero long-run dilution (21M cap). "The seemingly small difference in dilution between bitcoin and gold is bigger than it seems."
- **Layers of centralization (DeFi).** Not binary. Centralization hides at: compute layer (devs/validators/checkpoints), asset layer (custodial USDT/USDC/WBTC), data layer (oracles), interface layer (Infura/Alchemy web UIs), governance layer (whale voting). "DeFi's decentralization is largely marketing."
- **Work as arbiter of truth.** PoW anchors consensus to real-world physics (electricity/ASICs); PoS anchors only to coins inside the system → circular, requires slashing + "weak subjectivity" checkpoints from trusted parties. Cantillon/Adam Back: "money that doesn't have a cost ends up being political."
- **"Does it need a token?" test.** Most tokens exist as founder exit liquidity ("arbitrary seigniorage"), not function. If a token exists, suspect the motive.
- **Protocol winner-take-all vs. operating-system oligopoly.** Two plausible end-states: BTC ~90%+ as the protocol (like TCP/IP, SMTP) with smart contracts on L2; OR BTC ~30-50% with multiple coexisting platforms (like Windows/macOS/Linux). Determinant: regulatory environment (hostile → centralized chains die; permissive → fee-competing fragmentation).
- **Layered security / settlement model.** BTC base layer = Fedwire-equivalent wholesale settlement; Lightning/custodial layers handle retail. Not a scaling failure — architecture.

## Specific claims, mechanisms & data
- **Liquidity correlation:** BTC moved same direction as global M2 in **83% of 12-month rolling periods** (highest of any major asset); 74% on 6-month windows. Overall correlation (2013–2024) 0.94; 12-mo rolling avg 0.51; 6-mo 0.36. Ranking on 12-mo rolling correlation: BTC > gold > stocks > bonds. Global M2 = aggregate money supply of 8 largest economies, measured in USD (captures both dollar strength and credit creation).
- **Correlation breakdowns** occur on (1) idiosyncratic shocks (Mt. Gox, PlusToken, Terra/Luna, COVID) and (2) extreme valuations (high MVRV → supply-side selling overrides liquidity). Bullish-liquidity traders should still NOT long BTC at extreme MVRV.
- **Terra/Luna post-mortem:** Algorithmic UST/Luna mint-burn peg required Luna market cap to grow with UST supply; collateral ratio fell from ~200% toward dangerous lows in months; ~20% Anchor yields were VC-subsidized fake demand (Anchor reserve fell 40% in April alone). Collapse functioned like an EM currency crisis — a well-funded attacker short BTC simultaneously broke the peg ("George Soros vs. Bank of England"). Centralized: Terraform Labs manually lent LFG's BTC reserves to market makers. BTC took "a price hit"; Terra took "a kill shot."
- **2022 CeFi collapse mechanics:** GBTC premium→discount flip stranded "low-risk arbitrage" firms; 3AC = opaque uncollateralized leverage across many lenders; BlockFi stayed collateralized but FTX exposure killed it. Lesson is NOT "DeFi>CeFi" — it's "**over-collateralized lending is safer than under-collateralized lending.**" Biggest losses came from uncollateralized loans to opaque entities or custodying with them.
- **DeFi risk data:** >$2.5B exploited from smart contracts over 2.5 yrs; 97.7% of Uniswap-listed coins (Mar 2022) were rug-pulls; only ~3 of 20,000+ coins ever made sustained second-cycle highs. Synthetic stablecoins face an unsolvable problem (back low-vol asset with high-vol collateral); DAI added USDC as collateral in Mar 2020, importing the centralization it meant to displace. FTX-issued wrapped BTC/ETH on Solana de-pegged to ~zero. Oracles are unavoidable centralized chokepoints for any real-world price.
- **PoS/node centralization:** ETH PoS client ~100,000 lines vs PoW ~100; ETH ~6,700 nodes, ~2,400+ cloud-hosted (Amazon ~2,700). Solana validator needs 12 cores / 128GB RAM / 1Gbit up; 17-hr outage Sept 2021 needed coordinated restart. Tether blacklisted 500+ addresses. Custodial stablecoins ($140B by Nov 2021, two largest ~$115B) get **unilateral fork-selection power** — the fork they don't honor sees its DeFi rendered insolvent.
- **Bitcoin immutability proof (Block Size Wars 2015-17):** 80%+ hashpower + Bitmain + major devs + Coinbase/Grayscale (NYA) all pushed bigger blocks (SegWit2x) and ALL failed because individual node operators refused. In a PoS system those same corporates could have voted with staked coins. BTC's separation of miner/dev/node power prevented capture.
- **Ethereum economics:** Launched 72M pre-mined (BTC = 0), supply changed via multiple EIPs; dominant use case is circular ("operating system for moving around crypto tokens"). Late-2017→late-2020 USD value settled on ETH ~tripled (beat BTC) yet ETH market cap merely re-tested old highs — ecosystem growth ≠ token appreciation (predicted by John Pfeffer 2017). EIP-1559 "elegant" but: "given how many times Ethereum's monetary policy has changed, why assume EIP-1559 is permanent?" ETH full nodes already exceed BTC and grow faster; "super-full nodes" break the "don't trust, verify" property.
- **Corporate treasury data ($100, 1928–2023):** T-bills → $2,249; gold → $10,042; S&P500 (w/ divs) → $787,018. US broad money 380x ($54.8B→$20.8T) vs gold ~100x = ~3.8x cumulative dilution gap. BTC ~94% mined; 99%+ by mid-2030s. MSTR began Aug 2020, massive enterprise-value outperformance over 4 yrs; Tesla, Block, Semler, Metaplanet (3x since Apr-2024) followed. 2023 FASB rules eased crypto accounting. Best candidates: profitable, stagnant "value stocks" with no good reinvestment options and capital-markets access.
- **Bitcoin stocks/bonds:** Corporate leverage beats alternatives — multi-year bonds avoid the forced-liquidation risk of margin, and avoid leveraged-ETF volatility decay (2x BITU underperformed spot BTC despite BTC rising; alternating +10%/-10% days bleed leveraged ETFs). Capital-mandate problem: trillions of mandate-constrained capital (stock funds can only buy stocks) can only get BTC via equities like MSTR. Concentration ≠ control: PoW means holding coins grants no voting/censorship power (Mt. Gox held ~6% of supply, network fine).
- **Fee-based security model:** Target security budget ≈ 0.5–1.5% of market cap/yr. Early 2021: $1.85B fees on $740B cap = 0.25% (below target). Post-2024 halving inflation <1%, so fees must compensate. At ~100-120M tx/yr, $10-30/tx → $1-3B/yr. For a $10k+ transaction a $100 fee is ~1% — rational. BTC settles base-layer wholesale (120M+ tx/yr capacity); retail goes to Lightning/custodial. Hardest part of a 51% attack is acquiring majority ASICs (capital-intensive, un-hideable), not electricity. Satoshi: "in 20 years there will either be very large transaction volume or no volume."

## How to APPLY (decision rules for an agent using this lens)
1. **Among digital assets, treat BTC as the only investable monetary asset.** Everything else = speculation/equity; size accordingly. Reasonable allocations: 100/0 BTC/ETH (her own), 90/10 or 80/20 "not crazy."
2. **Use BTC as a macro liquidity gauge.** Bullish global M2 (rising, dollar weakening) = structural tailwind; falling = headwind. Expect ~83% directional alignment over 12-mo horizons; ignore the signal intraday/intra-month (noise).
3. **Overlay MVRV Z-Score before adding BTC.** Even with bullish liquidity, do NOT buy at extreme-high MVRV; low MVRV = accumulate. Crashes (2015/18/20/22) are rebalancing opportunities for 3-5yr holders.
4. **Screen tokens with the equity/Howey lens + "does it need a token?"** Red flags: unsustainable yields (~20% APY = Ponzi mechanics), central foundation dependency, declining collateral ratios, heavy VC dependence, pre-mine, mutable monetary policy.
5. **Treat yield-chasing in volatile crypto as a sucker's game** ("nickels in front of a steamroller"). Prefer over-collateralized exposure regardless of CeFi/DeFi label; counterparty/custody/oracle/smart-contract risk usually dwarfs the yield.
6. **For BTC equity exposure:** direct/ETF + self-custody for unconstrained individuals; treasury stocks for mandate-constrained institutions; leveraged names (MSTR) only for high-conviction holders who want better leverage mechanics than margin or leveraged ETFs — and remember leverage cuts both ways.
7. **For corporate treasury decisions:** BTC suits profitable, stagnant firms with 4+ yr horizons and volatility tolerance; partial position (build war chest gradually) mitigates risk; avoid "complex schemes" — buy BTC itself, not crypto-company equity (the FTX lesson).
8. **Watch the long-term security budget** as an adoption KPI: blocks consistently full of large transactions + healthy fee levels + geographic miner diversification = sustainable; fees stuck far below ~0.5% of cap with thin volume = the open risk.

## Caveats / where she hedges
- "All models are wrong, but some useful"; Saylor's "all your models are destroyed" — BTC is a paradigm shift, statistical relationships are imperfect; correlation ≠ causation (both BTC and M2 respond to central-bank policy).
- Liquidity correlation is strong only over annual+ horizons, weak short-term; dataset is only ~11 yrs / few monetary regimes; EM-equity comparison ran during a strong-dollar era.
- PoS is "not inherently bad" — fine for voluntary platforms (corporation-like) where users can exit; unsuitable as censorship-resistant global money. Smart-contract tokens "aren't worthless" — can hold speculative/utility value (gaming like Axie, stablecoin remittances) while failing as sound money. She was tactically bullish on ETH price (2021 supply squeeze) despite long-term reservations — price action can diverge from fundamentals for years.
- A small ETH position "isn't crazy" / can be "asymmetric speculation"; ETH 2.0 engineering is "clever"; she'd reconsider ETH after years of stable monetary policy + real non-speculative use cases. Solana has a "decent shot" in a non-hostile regulatory regime because users value cheap fees over decentralization.
- Treasury/leverage: BTC volatility "likely to continue to be high" for years; early-network tail risk "shrinks but persists"; corporate adoption rates unpredictable; "still mostly for visionaries." She co-maintains the bcap consensus-risk document (acknowledges legitimate monitoring needs).
- Fee security: "no firm number" on the right budget; Princeton 2016 paper flags potential fee-timing instability; the self-correcting equilibrium "could theoretically undershoot." It's "a boundary condition Bitcoin must navigate," not solved and not fatal.
