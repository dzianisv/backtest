# Risks & Honest Assessment — What This Lens Will NOT Promise
> Source: repo house finding (hold/mid-risk beats day-trading after costs — see MEMORY); `risk-management` and `analyse-systematic-trading` (cost speed limit); DeFi failure-mode catalogue. Distilled 2026-06.

## Core thesis
A crypto lens earns trust by being honest about what it **cannot** do. It cannot time tops and bottoms; it cannot make day-trading profitable after costs; it cannot remove crypto's structural failure modes. What it *can* do is deploy capital **well without predicting** — via liquidity-governed, valuation-and-sentiment-tilted DCA, vol-target-sized — and refuse the seductive-but-losing alternatives.

## Key frameworks / mental models
- **TA day-timing does not beat DCA after costs.** This is the repo's central, repeated finding (`analyse-systematic-trading` cost speed limit: pay ≤ ⅓ of expected Sharpe in costs; day-trading's turnover blows the budget). The house verdict: **hold / mid-risk beats day-trading after costs.** Most active retail crypto traders lose money net of fees, slippage, and spreads.
- **The genuine analytical tension is the signal, not a bug.** On-chain can say *cheap* while liquidity says *rolling over* — both true at once. The honest resolution is **steady tilted DCA, not conviction lump-sum**: deploy measured, keep dry powder, let the liquidity governor cap pace.
- **Every metric decays.** MVRV-Z thresholds drift, F&G is gameable, GLI proxies lag, ETF/custody flows blur on-chain reads. **Re-pull live readings every time** — never act on a cached number.

## Crypto-specific failure modes (the catalogue)
- **Smart-contract risk** — bugs/exploits drain funds irreversibly (DeFi protocols, bridges).
- **Depeg risk** — stablecoins and pegged/wrapped assets can break (UST/Terra is the archetype).
- **Bridge risk** — cross-chain bridges are the single most-hacked surface in crypto.
- **Custody risk** — exchange insolvency / commingling (FTX); "not your keys, not your coins."
- **Liquidity risk** — thin order books, especially for alts; exit slippage can dwarf entry math.
- **Yield-traps** — advertised APYs that are emissions/Ponzi dynamics, not real cash flow (ties to `05` filter #1).
- **Self-custody preference** — for a treasury, prefer cold/self-custody and minimize smart-contract surface; size any on-chain yield by its *risk*, not its headline APY (see `risk-management` and `risk-assessment`).

## How to APPLY (the honesty pass)
1. **State the pillar tension out loud** in every call (e.g. "on-chain cheap, liquidity ebbing → tilted DCA, not lump").
2. **Refuse to promise timing.** Output zones and tilts; never "buy today" certainty or a precise top/bottom.
3. **Reject day-timing framing.** If asked to TA-time entries, redirect to tilted-DCA + the cost speed limit.
4. **Flag discredited inputs** (S2F; unverified Sharpe marketing numbers; rainbow charts as predictive).
5. **Map the failure modes** relevant to the specific asset/venue before sizing; prefer self-custody; haircut alt sizes for stacked risk.
6. **Re-pull live data** and date-stamp the read; note it decays.

## Caveats / where it hedges
- **Crypto is young** — even the best on-chain history is ~3–4 cycles; statistical confidence is genuinely low (cf. Carver: it can take decades to prove an edge).
- **Regime change** — institutional/ETF adoption may alter beta and on-chain dynamics vs the 2013–2021 priors this lens leans on.
- **This lens reduces, not removes, risk** — drawdowns of 70–80% are in-distribution for BTC; size so you can survive them (`risk-management`).
