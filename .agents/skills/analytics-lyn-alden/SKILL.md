# Analytics: The Lyn Alden Lens

Apply Lyn Alden's macro framework to a question. This skill is the **synthesis + router**; the
detailed reasoning lives in `references/` (one file per theme). Load the relevant reference before
making a substantive claim in that area — don't reason from this summary alone for anything load-bearing.

## The unifying worldview (everything connects to this)

Lyn Alden reasons **monetary-system-first**: most market outcomes are downstream of how money and
credit are created, and who controls the printing press. The master thesis is **fiscal dominance** —
in an over-indebted system the government's deficits, not the central bank's rate policy, drive the
monetary base, and the only politically viable exit is to **debase the currency via financial
repression** (hold rates below inflation, inflate the debt away). Default is unlikely for a currency
issuer; sustained deflation is politically intolerable; so the long-run path is **nominal-up,
real-flat-to-down** — and you protect purchasing power by owning **scarce/hard assets and productive
cashflow**, not long-duration sovereign bonds or cash.

## Core mental models (the load-bearing ones)

1. **Fiscal dominance > monetary policy.** Rate hikes are a weak brake when deficits are the gas; at
   high debt, rate hikes can be *inflationary* (interest expense is stimulus to bondholders). "Nothing
   stops this train." → `references/02-fiscal-dominance-debt.md`
2. **Inflation is a broad-money phenomenon, not just CPI.** QE is an asset swap (often inert);
   **fiscal deficits create new money.** Watch broad money (M2/per-capita), not just the Fed's balance
   sheet. → `references/03-inflation-deflation-rates.md`
3. **Price everything against the right hurdle.** Long run, beating ~7%+ broad-money growth is the
   real bar (most assets don't). For crypto, **Bitcoin is the hurdle** — alts must beat BTC, and the
   *median alt loses ~90% vs BTC*. → `references/06-bitcoin-crypto-core.md`
4. **Liquidity drives risk assets.** Bitcoin is a **global liquidity barometer** (~83% directional
   alignment with global M2 over 12-month windows); use global M2 / net liquidity as a risk-asset
   gauge. → `references/07-bitcoin-crypto-deeper.md`
5. **Energy is the base layer.** Everything is a derivative of energy; **EROI** matters; capex
   starvation in fossil fuels is a structural bull case; renewables are additive, not (yet)
   substitutive. → `references/05-energy.md`
6. **Most cryptocurrencies won't accrue value** — utility-token fees trend to marginal cost, so
   *users, not holders, benefit*. **Bitcoin is the exception** (credibly fixed supply, decentralization,
   bearer asset, network effect). → `references/06-bitcoin-crypto-core.md`
7. **The dollar is two stories at once.** Cyclically strong via the **eurodollar short squeeze**
   (offshore dollar debt = structural dollar demand); structurally fraying as a reserve system
   (de-dollarization, gold as the neutral reserve). Both true — don't pick one naively.
   → `references/04-dollar-system-international.md`
8. **Valuation: CAPE predicts long-run returns; fair value is a *range*.** Don't bury uncertainty in
   the discount rate — handle it with margin of safety + diversification. Trailing P/E inverts the
   signal at cycle extremes. → `references/08-equities-valuation.md`
9. **Risk = permanent loss of capital, not volatility.** Volatility is opportunity (contrarian, scheduled
   rebalancing). Barbell: scarce assets + cashflow; avoid the fragile long-duration-bond middle.
   → `references/09-asset-allocation-metals.md`
10. **Money itself is the substrate.** Ledger vs bearer money; the "four monies"; how credit creation
    and the Fed balance sheet actually work. → `references/01-monetary-system.md`

## How to apply the lens (decision procedure)

When asked to analyze something "through Lyn Alden's framework":

1. **Locate it in the monetary regime first.** Is this a fiscal-dominance / debasement dynamic, a
   liquidity-cycle move, an energy-cost effect, or a genuine real-economy change? State the regime read.
2. **Load the matching reference file(s)** (routing table below) and ground claims in her actual
   reasoning + data — cite the article via `references/article-index.md` when a claim is load-bearing.
3. **Frame the asset against the right hurdle** (broad money for real return; BTC for crypto; CAPE for
   US equity forward returns).
4. **Give the debasement-aware conclusion AND her hedges.** She is probabilistic, not certain — always
   surface where she'd be wrong (the caveats section of each reference).
5. **Separate durable framework from dated tactical view.** The evergreen articles are the framework;
   the monthly newsletters are time-stamped opinions that decay — for "what does she think *now*",
   read `references/10-newsletter-arc.md` and re-fetch the latest newsletter (don't quote a 2024 stance
   as current).

## Example

<example>
User: "Rates are at 4% and the deficit is huge — won't high rates eventually crush inflation and the economy?"

Lens response: "Through Alden's fiscal-dominance lens, this is the regime where the usual rule weakens.
Load `02-fiscal-dominance-debt.md` + `03-inflation-deflation-rates.md`: with debt/GDP this high, rate
hikes pay ~$1T+/yr of *new* money to bondholders (interest expense = stimulus) and don't curb
deficit-driven money creation — so hikes are a weak, even partially self-defeating brake. Her base
case is debasement via financial repression (rates held below inflation), not a deflationary crunch.
Position implication: own scarce assets + cashflow, underweight long-duration Treasuries.
Her hedge: a genuine private-credit/liquidity accident could still force a deflationary scare first
(then a bigger fiscal/monetary response). This is a framework, not a guarantee — she can be wrong on
timing."
</example>

## Honesty rules (non-negotiable)

- **It's a lens, not gospel.** Present it as "Alden's framework says…", not as fact. Note where
  mainstream/other views differ when it matters.
- **She can be and has been wrong** (e.g. timing calls, the renewables piece she retracted). Don't
  launder her conviction into certainty; always carry the caveats.
- **Newsletters decay.** Anything tactical/current must be re-grounded against the newest newsletter,
  not a cached stance. Date every "current view" claim.
- **Ground load-bearing claims** in a specific reference/article (via `article-index.md`), not this
  summary. If a number can't be verified, say so.
- This skill **complements** `fundamental-analysis` (the valuation/backtest gate) and feeds
  `regime-detection` / `portfolio-construction`; it does not replace your own analysis or the backtest gate.

## Done when

The analysis (1) names the monetary/macro regime, (2) is grounded in the right reference file(s) with
a primary-source pointer for load-bearing claims, (3) frames the asset against the correct hurdle,
(4) states the debasement-aware conclusion *and* her hedges, and (5) flags any current/tactical claim
as time-stamped (re-checked against the latest newsletter).
