## The durable throughline (what never changes)
The spine of every newsletter is **fiscal dominance**: structural US deficits (~7% of GDP *before* any new stimulus) are large enough that fiscal policy, not the Fed, drives the economy. She dates the US entering this regime to ~2019. Six reasons it can't be stopped (Sep 2024): unbalanced Social Security, healthcare cost inefficiency, foreign/military spend, rising interest on accumulated debt, political polarization (neither party will cut), and tax receipts financialized to the stock market (austerity is self-defeating). Metaphor: Walter White's "Nothing stops this train. Nothing."

Consequences that recur in every issue:
- **Persistent currency debasement** — a five-decade structural trend, not a 2025 fad. Money supply compounds faster than bond yields over time.
- **Own scarce/hard assets, not long-duration fiat claims.** Bonds are the thing to avoid (they had their "worst 5-year period ever," 2020–2025).
- **Valuation still matters** — debasement does NOT guarantee soaring real returns; asset selection separates winners from "lost decades."
- **The Triffin dilemma** — reserve-currency status forces structural deficits; long-term USD headwind.
- **Three north stars never to fade** (Jan 2025): energy density, computation/AI, network effects.
- **The three-pillar portfolio** replaces 60/40: (1) profitable equities, (2) commodities/producers + hard money (gold, BTC), (3) cash/short bills. Each pillar wins in a different regime (disinflationary growth / inflation-stagflation / deflationary contraction).

## How to APPLY (decision rules)
1. **Default to the three-pillar mix**, not 60/40. Don't be long-duration-bond heavy.
2. **Tilt within hard assets toward under-owned + cheap**; don't chase euphoric momentum (her standing rule: don't call exact tops, but trim what has gone from undervalued to fairly valued — e.g. PMs after the Feb-2026 spike).
3. **BTC + gold = core debasement hedge**; size BTC to survive volatility; self-custody first.
4. **Hold cash/short bills as the deflation/volatility shock absorber**, not for yield.
5. **Selection > beta**: in fiscal dominance, nominal gains can mask real losses — owning the *right* scarce assets is the whole game.
6. **Escalation tripwire**: if repo/swap usage spikes, MOVE breaks, or yields surge on forced sovereign selling → the "gradual print" can flip to "big print"; lean harder into hard assets and away from fiat claims.
7. **Diversify custody/jurisdiction** as a structural (not tactical) hedge.
