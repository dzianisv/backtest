# Defensive vs Enterprising Investor & the Selection Rules — Graham's Framework
> Source: *The Intelligent Investor* ch. 4–7 (the two programs), ch. 14 ("Stock Selection for the Defensive Investor" — the 7 criteria), ch. 15 ("Stock Selection for the Enterprising Investor"), and the net-current-asset / "net-net" method from ch. 15 + *Security Analysis*; the Graham number is the textbook formalization of his ch. 14 P/E×P/B ≤ 22.5 rule. Distilled 2026-06-07. All reachable.

## Core thesis
Graham insists there is **no middle path**: you are either a **defensive (passive) investor** who wants a sound, low-maintenance portfolio and adequate results, or an **enterprising (active) investor** willing to put in real, expert effort for a premium return. The fatal error is the **half-effort middle** — doing a little work and expecting outperformance, which reliably earns *speculative* results. The defensive investor follows simple, mechanical rules and a quality screen; the enterprising investor hunts **statistical bargains** the market has mispriced. Both are governed by margin of safety and diversification; they differ only in effort and in where the edge comes from (quality-at-a-fair-price + simplicity vs. mispricing exploited by labor).

## Key frameworks / mental models
**Defensive investor — the 7 stock-selection criteria (ch. 14):**
1. **Adequate size** — exclude small/fragile companies (a revenue/asset floor, inflation-adjusted).
2. **Strong financial condition** — current ratio ≥ 2; long-term debt ≤ net current assets (working capital).
3. **Earnings stability** — positive earnings in each of the last 10 years.
4. **Dividend record** — uninterrupted dividends for ≥ 20 years.
5. **Earnings growth** — ≥ ~⅓ growth in per-share earnings over 10 years (3-yr averages, start vs end).
6. **Moderate P/E** — price ≤ 15× average earnings of the last 3 years.
7. **Moderate P/B** — price ≤ 1.5× book value.
- **Combined cap (criteria 6×7):** P/E × P/B ≤ **22.5**. This is the seed of the **Graham number** = √(22.5 × EPS × BVPS) — the maximum *defensive* price for a stock.

**Enterprising investor — statistical bargains:**
- **Net-nets / NCAV:** Net Current Asset Value = current assets − *total* liabilities (ignore fixed assets entirely). Buy when price < **⅔ of NCAV** — you pay less than conservative liquidation-grade working capital and get the operating business for free.
- **Bargain hunting in baskets:** the edge is statistical; hold a *diversified* group (Graham/Graham-Newman ran ~30+), expecting winners to swamp the value traps.
- **Other enterprising fields Graham allowed:** unpopular large caps (relative-low-P/E), special situations / workouts (merger arbitrage), and secondary issues at a discount.

## Specific claims, mechanisms & data
- **Graham-Newman track record:** the net-net strategy returned ~20%/yr over ~30 years (1936–1956 era) per Graham's own account — the empirical backbone of the method.
- **"Don't try to be smart, be disciplined":** the defensive 7-criteria portfolio is meant to be re-checked rarely (annually), with low turnover — outperformance comes from *not* trading, costs, and taxes.
- **Adequate diversification spec:** Graham suggested the defensive investor hold ~10–30 names across major industries; concentration is for the enterprising investor who has truly done the work.
- **The "two-thirds of NCAV" margin** is itself the margin of safety expressed on the balance sheet — the discount to already-conservative (liquidation-biased) asset value.

## How to APPLY (decision rules for an agent using this lens)
1. First decide the program honestly: defensive (rules + simplicity) or enterprising (real labor). Refuse the half-effort middle.
2. **Defensive:** screen with the 7 criteria; reject any name failing financial-strength (#2) or earnings-stability (#3); cap price via the Graham number (√(22.5×EPS×BVPS)); hold 10–30 names, low turnover, annual review.
3. **Enterprising:** build a *diversified basket* of statistical bargains — net-nets below ⅔ NCAV and/or sub-Graham-number quality names — never a single bet; verify the balance sheet is real and not melting.
4. Use the Graham number as a *price ceiling*, not a target — it's the max a defensive investor should pay, not an estimate of fair value.
5. Always combine selection with diversification and margin of safety — the rules underperform if applied to one or two concentrated picks.

## Caveats / where he hedges
- **The 1970s thresholds are calibrated to that market.** Modern asset-light, high-ROIC firms routinely trade above P/B 1.5 and P/E 15 for good reason; the strict 7-criteria screen now skews to deep-cyclicals, financials, and ex-growth names and excludes most quality compounders (the gap Buffett/Munger closed by adding *qualitative* quality — see `analytics-warren-buffett`).
- **Net-nets are nearly extinct** in developed large/mid caps after decades of the strategy being known; they survive mainly in micro-caps, distressed names, and some Asian markets — and there they're often cheap *for a reason* (see `05-applying-graham-today.md`).
- **The Graham number breaks for negative-book / negative-EPS firms and asset-light businesses** — it's undefined or misleading where the value isn't on the balance sheet.
- Statistical value can **underperform for long stretches** (the 2010s growth regime); the method needs patience and the basket, not a single cycle.
