# Applying Graham Today — How the Timeless Rules Map to 2026
> Graham died in 1976, so there is no "current views" file. This is how his fixed frameworks translate to today's market — the time-decaying layer. Source: Graham's 1976 FAJ interview (his own late-life update); the academic value-factor literature (Fama-French HML, the 2010s value drawdown); practitioner net-net studies. Distilled 2026-06-07; re-check the value-vs-growth regime claims against current data.

## What still holds (evergreen, do not discount)
- **Investment vs speculation, margin of safety, Mr. Market, temperament-over-IQ, diversification, rules-based allocation + rebalancing.** These are about human behavior and risk, not about any market era — they are as binding in 2026 as in 1949. The crypto and AI-equity manias are textbook Mr. Market.
- **"Price ≠ value" and "weighing machine in the long run"** remain the operating premise of all fundamental investing.
- **Mechanical rebalancing inside bounded allocation** is now mainstream best practice (target-date funds, risk-parity, the 60/40) — Graham's ch. 8 idea, vindicated.

## What has decayed (handle with care, time-stamp)
- **Classic net-nets are nearly extinct** in developed large/mid caps. Decades of the strategy being public, plus better disclosure and activist liquidation of cash-rich shells, arbitraged them away. They persist mainly in micro-caps, distressed names, and some Asian markets (Japan/Korea) — and there they are often cheap *for governance/structural reasons* (trapped cash, no catalyst). Treat any surviving net-net basket as a niche micro-cap strategy, not a core allocation.
- **The 7 defensive criteria are 1970s-calibrated** to an industrial, asset-heavy market. P/B ≤ 1.5 and P/E ≤ 15 now systematically exclude asset-light, high-ROIC compounders (software, platforms, brands) where the value isn't on the balance sheet. Modern application: keep the *spirit* (financial strength, earnings stability, paying a moderate price) but recognize the strict thresholds screen mostly cyclicals, financials, energy, and ex-growth names.
- **The Graham number** is undefined/misleading for negative-book or asset-light firms — it's a balance-sheet tool for tangible-asset businesses.
- **"High-grade bonds" assumed positive real yields.** Through the 2010s ZIRP era bonds offered negative real yield; the allocation principle held but the instrument needed substitutes (short duration, TIPS, cash, quality equity).

## The big honest hedge: value's lost decade
- **Pure statistical/deep value (HML) materially lagged growth from roughly 2007–2020**, the worst and longest value drawdown on record, before a partial 2021–2024 mean-reversion. A strict Graham screen would have largely missed the FAANG/AI compounding that drove index returns. This is the central modern critique of the rules-based path.
- **The value school's own response** was Buffett & Munger's migration from Graham's "cigar butts" to "a wonderful business at a fair price" — adding *qualitative quality* (moats, ROIC, management) to Graham's *quantitative cheapness*. Use `analytics-warren-buffett` as the evolved branch; use this Graham skill for the rules-based, balance-sheet, margin-of-safety floor.

## Graham's own last word (1976 FAJ interview)
- He had grown skeptical that **elaborate individual security analysis** still beat the market after costs for most investors, and leaned toward **a few simple rules + broad diversification** — an early, honest gesture toward what became **index investing**. For most people pursuing the *defensive* path today, a low-cost broad index fund inside a bounded, rebalanced stock/bond allocation is the faithful modern expression of Graham's defensive program; reserve the *enterprising* net-net/deep-value work for those who will genuinely do it, in baskets, in the corners where mispricing still lives.

## How to APPLY today (decision rules)
1. Lead with the evergreen behavioral core (margin of safety, Mr. Market, allocation + rebalancing) — apply it verbatim.
2. Translate, don't transplant, the screens: keep financial-strength and moderate-price spirit; relax the literal P/B 1.5 / P/E 15 for businesses whose value is intangible, and say so.
3. Confine net-nets to a small micro-cap/distressed/Asia basket sleeve, flagged as niche and governance-risky — never a core holding.
4. For a default defensive investor, recommend low-cost index + bounded rebalanced allocation as Graham's own late-life logic, not as a betrayal of it.
5. Pair with `analytics-warren-buffett` for the qualitative-quality overlay; time-stamp every value-vs-growth-regime claim and re-check it against current factor data.
