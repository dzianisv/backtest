# Principles for Decision-Making & Dalio's Current Positioning — Ray Dalio's Framework
> Source: Dalio, *Principles: Life and Work* (2017; principles.com); his decision-making essays; current-positioning from Davos/CNBC (Jan 2025), Fox Business & NPR (Jul 2025), World Governments Summit Dubai (Feb 2026, Fortune 4 Feb 2026), Fortune 14 Mar 2026, and reporting on gold replacing Treasuries (stocktwits/Yahoo Finance, late 2025–early 2026). Distilled 2026-06-07. Every current claim is dated below.

## Core thesis
Good outcomes come from a **disciplined decision process**, not from being smart. Treat life and markets as a machine that produces outcomes you can learn from: encounter reality, feel the pain of mistakes, reflect, and distill principles so you handle the next case better — **Pain + Reflection = Progress**. Make decisions by **radical open-mindedness** and **believability-weighting** (trust the views of those with track records and sound logic over your own ego), and think in **expected value / probabilities**, not certainties. Applied to investing, the throughline is **diversification above all**: because you can't predict the future, own uncorrelated assets across environments, hold gold and non-US-dollar exposure as protection against debt/currency debasement, and don't sit in cash (it quietly loses to inflation). As of 2024–2026 he is loudly defensive on US debt, constructive on gold, wary of US Treasuries and the dollar, and focused on US–China "capital war" and AI as a swing force.

## Key frameworks / mental models
- **Pain + Reflection = Progress**: mistakes are data; reflect honestly to extract a principle; embrace pain as feedback.
- **Radical open-mindedness**: replace the attachment to being right with the joy of finding what's true; actively seek out and stress-test the strongest opposing view.
- **Believability-weighted decision making / idea meritocracy**: weight opinions by the person's (1) repeated track record at the thing and (2) ability to logically explain cause→effect. Not one-person-one-vote, not dictatorship.
- **Think in expected value & probabilities**: weigh probability × payoff; be willing to be wrong on individual calls if the process has positive EV; "make your worst-case acceptable."
- **The machine view / two-level thinking**: separate "you the designer" from "you the worker"; look down on your situation as a system to be engineered.
- **Diversification mantra**: ~15 uncorrelated bets (file 04); own gold and other currencies/real assets as debasement insurance; "cash is trash" over time because inflation erodes it.

## Specific claims, mechanisms & data (current positioning — DATED)
- **Gold allocation**: advises investors hold **~10–15% in gold**, calling it "the most sound fundamental investment" and "a very effective diversifier to debt and equity." (Repeated 2024–2026.)
- **Gold > Treasuries as the riskless asset**: (late 2025) said gold has "started replacing some US Treasury holdings as the riskless asset"; (Davos Jan 2026) gold is effectively the **world's second-largest reserve asset after the dollar**.
- **US debt warnings**: (Jul 2025, Fox Business/NPR) likened the US fiscal path to an "economic heart attack"; "we're spending ~40% more than we're taking in." The **3% solution** — cut the deficit from ~6–7% to ~3% of GDP (Davos Jan 2025).
- **Treasuries/dollar risk**: "the biggest risk is that debt assets like US Treasuries will be defaulted on or devalued — more likely devalued"; advises **reducing overexposure to US-dollar-denominated assets**, adding international equities and real assets.
- **"Capital war"**: (World Governments Summit, Dubai, Feb 2026) "We are on the brink" of a "capital war" — countries attacking each other via control of money/capital flows (sanctions, capital controls, seizures).
- **Big Cycle Stage 5**: (Fortune, 14 Mar 2026) "We are now in Stage 5… the most dangerous phase," analogous to "pre-1945 times."
- **AI**: names AI/technology as a primary force in the "Overall Big Cycle" — a productivity/power swing factor that can either offset or accelerate the debt/disorder dynamics (per *How Countries Go Broke*, 2025).
- **"Cash is trash"**: said at Davos Jan 2018 and reiterated Jan 2020 — cash loses to inflation over time; he prefers diversified real/uncorrelated assets.

## How to APPLY (decision rules for an agent using this lens)
1. For any call, write the **probabilities and payoffs** (expected value), not a point prediction; ensure the worst case is survivable.
2. **Believability-weight** inputs: upweight sources with a real track record AND a logical cause-effect explanation; explicitly seek the best opposing case before deciding (radical open-mindedness).
3. After any loss, run **Pain + Reflection = Progress**: log the mistake, extract a reusable principle, update the system.
4. Default to **diversification across uncorrelated assets and environments**; do not concentrate in one asset, currency, or country.
5. Hold a **gold / real-asset sleeve (~10–15%)** as debasement and geopolitical insurance; treat long-dated US-dollar nominal claims as carrying devaluation risk in the current regime (per dated 2025–26 views).
6. Don't park long-term capital in **cash** as a strategy — it bleeds to inflation; use it tactically only.
7. Factor **capital-war / jurisdiction risk** (sanctions, controls, seizure) into custody and country allocation, and weigh **AI** as a productivity wildcard when assessing growth.

## Caveats / where he hedges
- **"Cash is trash" timing miss**: said it at Davos in Jan 2020 just before the COVID crash, when cash briefly was the best asset; cash also outperformed in 2022. Dalio frames it as a *long-run* statement, but the short-run calls were poorly timed.
- **Perennial debt-crisis early calls**: he has warned of a US debt crisis for years without it breaking; he concedes timing is hard and "nothing is predestined."
- His own **All Weather lost ~22% in 2022** (file 04) — the diversification gospel failed in a stocks-and-bonds-down regime.
- Principles' management culture (radical transparency, public ratings) is **divisive**; critics call it harsh/cultish and note believability-weighting can entrench insiders.
- His current gold/anti-Treasury stance is a **view, not consensus**; many argue the dollar's network effects and US strengths keep Treasuries the global safe asset.
